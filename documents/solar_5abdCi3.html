Facts About the Sun

  • Type of Star: The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V), also known as a yellow dwarf.
  • Composition: The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%), with trace amounts of other elements.
  • Temperature:
    • The Sun's core temperature reaches about 15 million°C (27 million°F).
    • The surface temperature (photosphere) is approximately 5,500°C (9,932°F).
  • Size and Mass:
    • The Sun's diameter is about 1.39 million kilometers (864,000 miles).
    • It contains 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
  • Energy Production: The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium in its core.
  • Light Travel Time: Light from the Sun takes approximately 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  • Age: The Sun is estimated to be 4.6 billion years old and is halfway through its life cycle.
  • Rotation: The Sun rotates on its axis approximately once every 25 days at the equator and once every 36 days at the poles (differential rotation).
  • Solar Activity:
    • The Sun has an 11-year solar cycle, marked by varying levels of solar activity like sunspots and solar flares.
    • Solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs) can affect Earth's magnetosphere, causing geomagnetic storms.
  • Role in the Solar System:
    • The Sun’s gravity holds the solar system together.
    • It provides the energy necessary for life on Earth through sunlight.
