The Sun

The Sun is the center of the solar system and the primary source of energy for life on Earth. It is a giant ball of hot plasma and accounts for 99.86% of the solar system's mass.

The Planets

  • Mercury: The smallest planet, closest to the Sun.
  • Venus: Known as Earth's twin due to its similar size.
  • Earth: The only planet known to support life.
  • Mars: Known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich surface.
  • Jupiter: The largest planet, famous for its Great Red Spot.
  • Saturn: Known for its stunning ring system.
  • Uranus: A gas giant with a bluish color due to methane in its atmosphere.
  • Neptune: The farthest planet, known for its deep blue color.

Fun Facts

Did you know?

  • A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus!
  • Jupiter has over 75 moons.
  • Pluto was reclassified as a "dwarf planet" in 2006.
  • Saturn's rings are made mostly of ice particles, with some rock debris.
